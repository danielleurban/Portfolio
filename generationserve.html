User Interview & Survey Insights

Each team member conducted one user interview, and then we came together to discover commalities between the diverse group of parents we talked to and create an affinity diagram. We used our insights to then create an empathy map and user persona.

Our Research did 3 Key Things

First, it validated our assumption that parents want to volunteer with their kids. We found that 81% of parents we talked to and surveyed valued volunteering with their kids.

Second, it showed us parents priorities when looking for somewhere to volunteer. These include transparency, organization, and an impact on community.

Third, it uncovered key pain points parents experince when trying to volunteer with their kids. This validated the value of what Generation Serve provides through their family volunteering calendar and gave us insight into what to prioritize in our re-design.

Usability Testing Plan

After Understanding the needs of our users and stakeholders, we created a usability testing plan and conducted four tests on mobile as well as desktop. We narrowed down our focus using the "I like I wish I wonder" framework and created a feature prioritization matrix before moving foward.

Task 1

Task 2

Feature Prioritization

After going through a feature prioritization exercize, we came up with three main areas of the site to focus on.

1

Home Page

How might we include the most relevant information on the home page and tell a story about the value of Generation Serve?

2

Registration Process

How might we the registration process to keep users engaged?

3

Calendar Interface

How might we make the mobile calander more user friendly by adding the ability to filter events and creating hieracrchy in the design?
