Generation Serve Website Redesign
About the Project
Background

Generation Serve’s mission is to develop generations of community-minded leaders. They do this through family volunteering activities and programs such as service learning and teen leadership.

Our task as a group of four bootcamp students was to discover current problems with the website and create a redesign that met both user and business needs.

  • Timeline

    3 Weeks

  • Role

    UX Designer

    UI Designer: Calendar Interface

  • Tools

    Miro, Figma

Problem

Generation Serve is an events based organization. COVID-19 caused them to lose 50% of their monthly recurring donations and more than 50% of their staff.

Solution

Our redesign will increase user engagement, streamline processes, and create clear call to action statements to increase donations.

Hypothesis

If we improved the site navigation, and expanded the home page and Activities Calendar interfaces to include the most relevant information, as well as streamlining the registration process, it would lead to an increase in registrants, volunteers, and donors.

Success Metrics
You'll see more about this as I go into the user research, but we determined time on task as a metric to measure throughout our re-design due to our persona's busy family schedule and emphasis on efficiency.

Define:
“A problem well stated is a problem half solved.” -Charles Kettering
Our Research did Three Key Things

First, it validated our assumption that parents want to volunteer with their kids. We found that 81% of parents we talked to and surveyed valued volunteering with their kids.

Second, it showed us parents priorities when looking for somewhere to volunteer. These include transparency, organization, and an impact on community.

Third, it uncovered key pain points parents experience when trying to volunteer with their kids. This validated the value of what Generation Serve provides through their family volunteering calendar and gave us insight into what to prioritize in our redesign.

Usability Testing Plan

After understanding the needs of our users and stakeholders, we created a usability testing plan and conducted 4 tests on mobile and 4 on desktop.

We then narrowed down our focus using the “I like, I wish, I wonder” framework and created a feature prioritization matrix to get our priorities straight moving forward.

Feature Prioritization

After going through the feature prioritization exercise, we came up with three main areas of the site to focus on.

1

Home Page

2

3

Calendar Interface

How might we make the mobile calendar more user-friendly by adding the ability to filter events and creating hierarchy in the design?

Week 2:

Develop & Test:
"The only way to find out if it really works is to test it." -Steve Krug

We divided up our focus areas as a team, and I took on being the lead designer for the calendar interface. Below showcases some of my iterations for our redesign based on testing at each stage.

Testing Round 1: Current Site

Insights

1

Users asked about a way to search for events on mobile

Therefore I added filters for location, age range, and activity type.

2

Users were uncertain upon scanning which events were full and which had openings

Therefore I brainstormed ways to make the interface more scannable using padding, spacing, and color choice.

Testing Round 2: Sketched Ideas

Insights

1

Users didn't mind not seeing event info for full events

This validated my assumption that "closing" full event tiles would simplify the interface

2

It was unclear if the category tags were meant to be buttons or just displaying info

Therefore I put all info into a list with icons instead of using tags

Testing Round 3: Mid Fidelity

Insights

1

Users found the touch targets too small for the dropdowns

Therefore I dug into accessibility standards and enlarged the buttons

2

"The filter button feels unnecessary, the drop down should automatically filter"

3

Users were more likely to click into the event page because of this clear Call to Action

Testing Round 4: High Fidelity

Insights
1

Users responded well to the date filter

This helped us with our goal of cutting down information overload and decreasing time on task.

2

The current re-design made users find the event they were looking for 90% faster on desktop.

Retrospective

I learned so much through the course of this project about working on a team with other designers. By taking the lead on one of the website's key features, I learned to trust my own ideas and the importance of requesting feedback early and often from my team.

By going through several rounds of testing I learned that while trusting my ideas is important initially, I need to always be open to changing and adapting them based on data.
