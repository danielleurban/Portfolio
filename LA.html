UX Case Study: The Local Agenda
About the Project
Background

For my first project in my bootcamp, I was asigned a partner to do user research and problem identification with around the concept of travel. The initiail stages of research and our first iterations of onboarding are pulled from that project.

5 months later I revisisted the project in order to prototype key features, and do concept testing to further explore our Hypothesis.

Problem

People are ready to travel again after two years at home due to COVID-19. Young people feel open to solo travel because of the flexibility, but are worried about missing out on social experinces and local insightsthat come from from traveling with or to visit friends.

Solution

The Social Agenda is a travel app that will help solo travelers feel less intimated by a new place byconnecting with locals and and fellow travelers to discover unique experiences.

Hypothesis

We believe that people have a desire to travel alone, but are sometimes hesitant to take the leap to do so. People might be more confident to try solo travel if we made an app that could assist them in feeling safe, confident, and socially connectedwhile still having the benefits of traveling alone.

We might do this by creating tools for an organic way for people to discover local insight into activities, and meet up with people with similar interests

Success Metrics
Discover

Building Empathy and Understanding the Problem

We began by conducting 5 1x1 interviews with people in their early 20s to gather insight about general attitudes and habits around travel.

We discovered an openness and curiosity around the idea of solo travel and meeting new people, but a hesitancy about the uncertainties that come with being alone in a new place.

User Interview Insights
Survey Insights

After analysing our data from the 1:1 Interviews, we decided to narrow our focus on solo travel. We created a survey to better understand people’s attitudes around specifically solo travel.

We surveyed 31 people in between the ages of 18-30, and found an openness to solo travel,as well as some key pain points that are preventing people from trying it.

Pain Points

01

Uncertainty about what to do

02

Fear of being alone in a new place

03

Missing out on social connection

How might we create an app that assits solo travelers in feeling connected to people around them and have more confidence in finding out what to do alone?
